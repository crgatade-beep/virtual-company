## Definition
Setting long-term direction, competitive positioning, and resource allocation to win in the market over a multi-year horizon.

## Frameworks
1. **SWOT**: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
2. **Porter Five Forces**: Industry competitive forces
3. **Blue Ocean**: Create uncontested market space
4. **OKRs**: Objectives + Key Results (quarterly cadence)
5. **Moat analysis**: What can competitors not copy?

## Annual Strategy Cycle
1. Market scan: What changed this year?
2. Competitive analysis: Who is gaining/losing?
3. Capability assessment: What can we uniquely do?
4. Vision update: Where do we want to be in 3-5 years?
5. OKR setting: 3-5 objectives, 2-3 KRs each
6. Budget allocation: Capital, headcount, tooling
7. Quarterly reviews: Adjust based on learnings

## OKR Rules
- Max 3-5 objectives per quarter
- Max 3 KRs per objective
- KRs must be measurable (numeric or binary)
- No "launch project" as KR — use "ship X, achieve Y metric"
- All-hands review every quarter

## KPIs
- OKR achievement rate: 70%+ average
- Strategic initiative ROI: > 3x
- Market position movement: Top 3 in segment within 3 years
