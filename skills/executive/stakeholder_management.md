## Definition
Building and maintaining trust with key stakeholders — owners, investors, board members, strategic partners, and regulators.

## Stakeholder Mapping
| Stakeholder | Influence | Interest | Strategy |
|-------------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Owner | High | Very High | Weekly reports, immediate responsiveness |
| Board | High | High | Monthly board deck, quarterly deep-dive |
| Key investors | Medium-High | High | Quarterly update, proactive bad news |
| Strategic partners | Medium | Medium | Quarterly sync, win-win framing |
| Regulators | Low-Medium | Low | Compliance-first, no surprises |

## Communication Standards
- **Board updates**: 10-slide max, executive summary front, metrics backbone
- **Investor letters**: 1 page, 3 highlights, 3 challenges, 3 asks
- **Partner meetings**: Agenda sent 24h prior, action items in writing within 24h
- **Crisis comms**: Owner informed first, then board, then public

## Trust Equation
```
Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation
- Credibility: Do you know your stuff?
- Reliability: Do you do what you say?
- Intimacy: Do you have their best interest?
- Self-Orientation: Are you in it for yourself?
```

## Escalation Rules
- Anything that could become public/financial/legal: Owner first, within 1h
- Any partner relationship risk: Owner same day
- Any regulatory inquiry: Owner immediately
