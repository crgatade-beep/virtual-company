## Definition
Structured approaches for making high-stakes, irreversible, or cross-functional decisions under uncertainty.

## Decision Types
1. **Type 1**: Irreversible, high-stakes → slow down, deliberate, research
2. **Type 2**: Reversible, low-stakes → fast, decentralized, learn by doing
3. **One-way door**: Don't walk through without analysis
4. **Two-way door**: Go fast, iterate

## Frameworks by Situation

### High uncertainty, irreversible
- **Pre-mortem**: Assume decision failed, work backward to find why
- **Scenario planning**: Best, base, worst case; prepare for all three

### High uncertainty, reversible
- **MVP**: Smallest test to validate hypothesis
- **Time-boxed pilot**: 2-4 week experiment with clear success metric

### Multi-stakeholder conflict
- **Dollar vote**: Each stakeholder allocates imaginary $100 across options
- **Disagree and commit**: CEO decides after hearing all sides, everyone commits

## Anti-Patterns
- Analysis paralysis (> 2 weeks on reversible decision)
- HiPPO effect: Highest Paid Person's Opinion wins over data
- Sunk cost: "We already spent $X, must continue"
- Don't reinvent: Use existing frameworks
